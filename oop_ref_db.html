Final Reflection & Learning Outcomes

    I often had the impression that, depending on the business area, the database architecture is already foreseen. In other words, there are already a number of released database management systems which have some reputation and each of them has their own advantages in terms of flexibility, consistency, data redundancy, etc. Hence, depending on the nature of your business, the question which system to use, is already to some kind predicted. Nevertheless, the last day of the module, I also read more about graph storing and I think there is a general increasing trend for database engineers to move in this direction (GOTO, 2020). Why? I also agree that the world in general became more connected the last few decades (because of globalization and coordinated political decisions) and that therefore links appear more naturally. The fact, that graph store are schemaless, is more an advantage in this respect because the system can start to draw the edges and links and to see the patterns (a schema) in a whole structure afterwards. To be more precise, I think it is exactly one of these advantages of graph stores that it helps to find a schema into a complex and very interlinked structure. (Webber & Van Bruggen, 2020)
